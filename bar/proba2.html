Satan’s Whiskers

This is not your clichéd swanky London cocktail bar, but Satan’s Whiskers is full to the brim with character that oozes from its exposed bricks, taxidermy and hip-hop music. It’s either not much to look at from the outside or it has been geniusly decorated. There’s a small neon sign, stuck onto a grimy-painted frontage, showing the bar’s name. The outside walls are covered in graffiti and old posters.

Bar Swift

Swift is split into two areas, with a more casual buzzy layout on the ground floor and a relaxed – some would say sensual – lounge in the basement. Drinkers in the upstairs bar, which is creatively called the Upstairs Bar, can walk in and receive table service. There is a short cocktail menu in Swift’s Upstairs Bar, which is accompanied by a food menu of small bites, such as oysters, aimed at kicking off your evening of restaurant-going or a trip to the theatre.
